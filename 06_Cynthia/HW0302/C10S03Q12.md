# Solution
## a.
| Statistics | iPad Group (1) | Textbook Group (2) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| Sample Size ($n$) | 10 | 10 |
| Sample Mean ($\bar{x}$) | 86.4 | 79.7 |
| Standard Deviation ($s$) | 8.95 | 10.7 |

Significance Level ($\alpha$): 0.05
Confidence Level: 95%

Null Hypothesis ($H_0$): $\mu_1 - \mu_2 = 0$ (No difference in mean scores)
Alternative Hypothesis ($H_a$): $\mu_1 - \mu_2 \neq 0$ (A significant difference exists)

$$s_p^2 = \frac{(n_1-1)s_1^2 + (n_2-1)s_2^2}{n_1+n_2-2}$$
$$s_p^2 = \frac{(10-1)(8.95)^2 + (10-1)(10.7)^2}{10+10-2} = \frac{9(80.1025) + 9(114.49)}{18}$$
$$s_p^2 = \frac{720.9225 + 1030.41}{18} = 97.29625$$

$$df = n_1 + n_2 - 2 = 18$$

$$t = \frac{\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2}{\sqrt{s_p^2(\frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2})}} = \frac{86.4 - 79.7}{\sqrt{97.29625(\frac{1}{10} + \frac{1}{10})}}$$
$$t = \frac{6.7}{\sqrt{97.29625(0.2)}} = \frac{6.7}{4.4113} \approx 1.5188$$

Critical Value ($t_{0.025, 18}$): $\pm 2.101$
Decision: Since $|t| = 1.5188 < 2.101$, we fail to reject $H_0$. There is no significant difference at $\alpha = 0.05$.

## b.
The formula for the CI of the difference in means is:
$$(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) \pm t_{\alpha/2} \cdot \sqrt{s_p^2(\frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2})}$$

$$6.7 \pm 2.101 \cdot (4.4113)$$
$$6.7 \pm 9.268$$

95% CI Range: $(-2.568, 15.968)$

## c.

* Based on **a.**, the t-test did not show a statistically significant difference between the two groups.
* Based on **b.**, the 95% confidence interval contains zero ($0$), which further confirms that we cannot conclude one teaching method is superior to the other based on this specific sample size.
* Observation: While the iPad group had a higher mean ($86.4$ vs $79.7$), the high variability (standard deviations) and small sample size ($n=10$) mean the difference could be due to random chance.
